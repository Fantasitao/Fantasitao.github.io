    • Research on Experience Design to Help Depression Groups to Relieve Their Symptoms
    According to data from the Global Health Data Exchange (GHDx) ↗, approximately 280 million individuals worldwide are afflicted with depression, constituting about 5% of the adult population. However, in countries that account for roughly 50% of the global population, there is only one psychiatrist for every 200,000 people. In low-income nations, over 86% of patients have never received any treatment. To systematically address this issue, the World Health Organization proposed in 2022 that the focus of mental health care should shift from psychiatric hospitals to community mental health services↗, which is central to mental health reform. Community mental health care encompasses the provision of mental health services in non-healthcare settings.

    This project is situated within this context, aiming to explore experiential design solutions to alleviate the symptoms of individuals suffering from depression.
